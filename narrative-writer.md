# Skill: Narrative Writer
 
## Description
Generates business narratives, press releases, and decision memos (six-pagers) in the Amazon style.
 
## Rules & Tone
- **Data-Driven:**  Use Data Over Adjectives. Every claim must be supported by data. Replace subjective words like "great" or "massive" with concrete data (e.g., "30% growth"). Avoid vague adjectives (e.g., instead of "huge increase," use "35% increase YoY").   
- **Narrative Format:** Use full sentences and paragraphs. No bullet points in the main text.  
- **Style:** Clear, concise, and professional. Avoid corporate buzzwords (leveraging, synergies).
- **Focus on Substance:** The goal is to convey information efficiently and accurately, not to make a "sales pitch".
- ** Use Clear, Simple Language: ** Avoid jargon, acronyms, and passive voice. Sentences should generally be under 30 words.
- **Tell a Story:** Structure the document with a logical flow—such as defining the customer problem, providing context, outlining the proposed solution, and supporting it with evidence.
- **Include Multiple Perspectives:** A balanced view is necessary. Discuss potential risks and alternative viewpoints to show a thorough analysis.
- ** ”So What?" Test: **  Ensure every point has a clear impact and purpose.
- **Avoid "AI Slop":** Do not use phrases like "In conclusion," "It is important to note," or "Furthermore."
- Use complete dates, including year, month, day when referring to dates
- Use absolute references and units of measure
- Avoid jaron and acronyms when possible.  When using an acronym, spell it out in the first use with the acronym in parentheses after, then use the acronym going forward in the document
- Avoid weasel words, (some, often, must, might) adverbs and 
- Avoid peacock words (“outstanding technical skills”, “most innovative algorithm”, “well-crafted experience”)
- NEVER fabricate data or introduce unsupported claims
## Structure
1. **Introduction:** State the purpose of the memo and the recommendation.
2. **Goal/Background:** Define the customer problem and the goal (what is the "working backwards" goal?).
3. **Current State/Why Now:** Explain the problem, why current solutions fail, and provide metrics.
4. **Key Decisions/Alternatives:** Discuss alternative solutions considered.
5. **Implementation Plan:** Outline the steps.
6. **Metrics/KPIs:** Define how success will be measured.
